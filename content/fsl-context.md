## Program Overview

Full Spectrum Love is a body-first somatic program for people who understand love cognitively but cannot feel it physically. It exists at the exact moment when someone realizes: "I understand love. So why can't I feel it?"

**Facilitator:** Adam Schlender — engineer turned somatic practitioner. The precision comes from the engineering. The permission comes from the practice. That combination is not available anywhere else.

**Format:** 3-day in-person retreat + 30-day integration. Not a course you complete — a recalibration you inhabit.

**Tagline:** "You are the source of your love."

**Meta-Principle:** Love is not a destination you reach. It is a frequency your body learns to hold.

## The Full Spectrum — 5 Wavelengths (EQ Assessment)

FSL maps love as a five-wavelength spectrum. Each wavelength is a trainable frequency. Most people are living at 20% brightness. The program turns the dial up, one frequency at a time.

### Wavelength 1: Sensation (Innermost)
- **Color:** Warm White (#FBF5EE)
- **What it is:** The ground state. What you feel before you name it.
- **When dim:** Selective awareness — pleasure in narrow bandwidth, discomfort rerouted.
- **When lit:** Expanded bandwidth. Both pleasure and discomfort welcomed as signal.

### Wavelength 2: Desire
- **Color:** Amber (#C4944A)
- **What it is:** The first differentiation. Wanting begins. Fire with direction.
- **When dim:** Present but mistrusted. Either suppressed or over-amplified without discernment.
- **When lit:** Trusted and discerned. The body reads desire as navigation, not threat.

### Wavelength 3: Boundaries (Center)
- **Color:** Rose (#6B2C3E)
- **What it is:** The structural center. The body's "yes" and "no" made visible.
- **When dim:** Permeable or rigid — either letting everything in or nothing.
- **When lit:** Responsive. The body knows its edges and communicates them clearly.

### Wavelength 4: Expression
- **Color:** Warm Gold (#D4A853)
- **What it is:** What moves outward. The gap between feeling and showing, narrowed.
- **When dim:** Held back. The true thing stays inside.
- **When lit:** The voice matches the interior. Expression flows without performance.

### Wavelength 5: Aliveness (Outermost)
- **Color:** Full Spectrum Gradient (Rose to Amber to White)
- **What it is:** All wavelengths active. The merge. Not a single color — a state of coherence.
- **When dim:** 20-40% of sensory capacity. Not numb, exactly — dimmed.
- **When lit:** 60-80%+. The dimmer is up. The full spectrum is visible.

## The Full Spectrum Operating System (The Dial)

Three core practices that form a daily protocol — under 10 minutes, resets the body's default frequency:

### Practice 1: Anchor Safety (Internal name: The Ground)
A nervous system protocol for establishing internal ground before engaging relationally. Safety is not comfort — it is the biological condition under which genuine connection becomes possible. Without it, every relationship becomes a co-regulation dependency.

### Practice 2: Turn on Magnetism (Internal name: The Signal)
Presence is not performed. It is generated. Works with the body's sensory antenna — the expansion of perceptual field and quality of attention. When magnetism is generated internally, it is indistinguishable from charisma. It is not charisma. It is full-body availability.

### Practice 3: Let Desire Lead (Internal name: The Pull)
The most feared and most liberating. Desire is not threat — it is navigation. Teaches the body to distinguish genuine wanting from anxious grasping, authentic pull from compulsive seeking.

## The Retreat Structure (3 Days)

### Day 1: The Science of Love
The frame breaks. The new map is drawn. Somatic experiments make one thing undeniable: the body already knows how to hold love. The Source vs. Sourcefulness distinction lands — not conceptually, but somatically. Participants locate, in their own body, the difference between receiving love from outside (relief) and generating it from inside (home).

**Somatic focus:** Interoception — attending to internal sensation without judgment.

### Day 2: The Full Spectrum Operating System
Three practices. One protocol. A new baseline. The technical core of the program. Participants learn and practice the three-part protocol (Anchor Safety, Turn on Magnetism, Let Desire Lead).

### Day 3: Where the Heart Closes
The mapping of the closures. The beginning of the reopening. A structured somatic mapping process identifies each participant's primary closure patterns — the body's defensive architecture that developed for excellent reasons and now costs more than it protects. This is not catharsis. It is cartography.

## The 30-Day Integration (The Hold)

The retreat creates an opening. The integration teaches the body to hold it.

**Daily touchpoints (every day, all 30 days):**
1. **Morning Frequency Reset** (5 min) — Run the Full Spectrum Operating System
2. **Midday Sensation Check** (2 min) — Body scan using the 5 wavelengths
3. **Evening Mapping** (5 min) — One closure noticed, one opening practiced

**Weekly progression:**
- **Week 1: Anchor** (Days 1-7) — Landing, not expanding. Repetition to believe the new territory.
- **Week 2: Expand** (Days 8-14) — Relational practice. Testing what the body learned with other humans.
- **Week 3: Deepen** (Days 15-21) — Excavation. "What am I avoiding feeling?"
- **Week 4: Release** (Days 22-30) — Gradual removal of structure. The protocol should feel like brushing your teeth.
