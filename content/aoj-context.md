## Program Overview

Alchemy of Joy is Brent Freeman's flagship personal development program under Light Pump Labs. It is a 90-day cohort experience (including an in-person retreat) designed around the premise that joy is not something you find — it is something you practice. AOJ addresses the mind track of the Trinity (AOJ = Mind, FSL = Body, ESU = Heart).

**Facilitator:** Brent Freeman — serial entrepreneur, meditation teacher, joy practitioner. Brent's voice is warm, direct, and disarmingly honest. He reads every assessment that comes through. His P.S. in emails is genuine: "I read them all."

**Format:** 90-day cohort journey with retreat, daily practices, community accountability, and progressive self-disclosure.

## The 6 Priority Pillars (The Priority Field)

The AOJ assessment maps a participant's Priority Field — 6 pillars of life satisfaction, each with 2 sub-dimensions rated 1-10. Together they create an honest snapshot of where someone's joy is flowing and where it is blocked.

### Pillar 1: Love
- **Sub A: Self** — Your relationship with yourself. Self-regard, self-compassion, internal dialogue.
- **Sub B: Romantic** — Your intimate relationship. Connection, vulnerability, presence with a partner.
- **Color:** Terra (#c4786a)

### Pillar 2: Faith
- **Sub A: Self** — Trust in your own path, decisions, and inner knowing.
- **Sub B: Universe** — Trust in something larger. Connection to meaning, surrender, spiritual grounding.
- **Color:** Blue (#7a8aaa)

### Pillar 3: Health
- **Sub A: Mind** — Mental health, clarity, cognitive habits, stress management.
- **Sub B: Body** — Physical health, movement, nutrition, energy, sleep.
- **Color:** Sage (#6a8a5a)

### Pillar 4: Family
- **Sub A: Blood** — Relationships with biological family. Parents, siblings, children.
- **Sub B: Chosen** — Relationships with chosen family. Deep friendships, community bonds.
- **Color:** Amber (#b8843a)

### Pillar 5: Career
- **Sub A: Making Money** — Financial health, income, professional trajectory.
- **Sub B: Giving Back** — Purpose, contribution, legacy, impact beyond earning.
- **Color:** Steel (#5a7a88)

### Pillar 6: Community
- **Sub A: Personal** — Social life, belonging, connection outside of work.
- **Sub B: Professional** — Network, mentors, collaborators, professional relationships.
- **Color:** Mauve (#9a6a7a)

### Tier Descriptions (Per Sub-Dimension)

Each sub-dimension has 5 tiers of descriptive text written in Brent's voice:

| Tier | Score Range | Tone |
|------|------------|------|
| 0 | 1-2 | Honest acknowledgment — "This needs attention" |
| 1 | 3-4 | Emerging awareness — "You're starting to see it" |
| 2 | 5-6 | Active work — "You're in the middle of it" |
| 3 | 7-8 | Genuine strength — "This is working" |
| 4 | 9-10 | Luminous — "This is where your joy lives" |

There are 60 total subtexts (5 tiers x 2 subs x 6 pillars) plus 10 additional for tier boundary blending.

## The Wobbly Leg

The lowest-scoring pillar is called the **Wobbly Leg** — the pillar that is most holding someone back from overall joy. This is the pillar Brent would focus on first in a coaching conversation. Each wobbly leg comes with:

- A narrative insight explaining why this pillar matters
- A meditation recommendation from the AOJ curriculum
- A practical tool recommendation
- A prescription paragraph for the field assessment email

The wobbly leg insight is never shaming. It is an honest naming: "This is where the work is. And that is good news, because now you know where to look."

## The JOS Framework (Joy Operating System)

The JOS is the core assessment and tracking system within AOJ. It operates as a radar visualization across the 6 pillars, showing:

- Current state scores
- Historical progression
- Balance across dimensions
- The gap between strongest and weakest pillars

The JOS Radar is displayed on the participant dashboard and updates as they complete check-ins throughout the 90-day journey.

## The 90-Day Journey Structure

### Phase 1: Awakening (Days 1-30)
- Daily check-ins across all 6 pillars
- Introduction to the AOJ toolkit (20 tools)
- Core Narrative work begins
- Community pairing (accountability partners)
- Weekly group sessions with Brent

### Phase 2: Integration (Days 31-60)
- Deeper work on the Wobbly Leg
- SubScript identification and rewriting
- JQ (Joy Quotient) assessment at midpoint
- Forgiveness ceremony preparation
- Self-Eulogy first draft

### Phase 3: Expression (Days 61-90)
- Self-Eulogy refinement and Theme Song
- Public sharing and progressive disclosure
- 90-Day Challenge completion
- Community Board contributions
- Graduation and next steps

## Readiness Signals

People who are ready for AOJ typically exhibit one or more of these patterns:

- **The Successful but Unfulfilled** — Career is working, but something fundamental is missing
- **The Aware but Stuck** — They have done therapy, read the books, understand the concepts — but cannot translate knowing into feeling
- **The Post-Crisis Rebuilder** — A divorce, a health scare, a loss — and a readiness to rebuild with intention
- **The Joy-Adjacent** — They can see joy in others but cannot consistently access it themselves
- **The Repeat Seeker** — They have tried retreats, programs, workshops — and keep returning because the change does not stick

**What AOJ is NOT for:** People in active crisis who need clinical support. People who want to be fixed rather than to practice. People who are not willing to be honest about their numbers.

## Assessment Email Personalization

The field assessment email generates personalized content based on overall score tier:

| Score | Opening Tone |
|-------|-------------|
| 8+ | Luminous — genuine strength acknowledged, what's next |
| 6-7.9 | Momentum — honest gaps with real forward motion |
| 4-5.9 | Honest — the assessment itself was an act of courage |
| Below 4 | Courageous — the willingness to look is the prerequisite |

The email includes: pillar breakdown with bar charts, personalized field assessment, wobbly leg prescription, tool recommendations (one meditation + one practical), two CTAs (cohort + 1:1 coaching), and a P.S. from Brent.

Tier boundaries (scores within 0.3 of 4/6/8) get blended transitional language rather than hard cutoffs.
